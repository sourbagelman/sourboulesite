Episode: “First Rounds”

Mornings don’t wait for you to be ready.

They don’t check if you slept enough… or if you figured things out the night before.

They just… start.

Lights come on. Machines warm up. The first cup gets made before anyone really says a word.

And just like that—you’re responsible for how someone else’s day begins.

There’s always a first one.

Careful. Measured. A little unsure.

The kind of start where you’re paying attention to every detail because you’re not confident enough not to.

Everyone has that phase.

Where you’re learning… but you don’t know if you’re actually getting better.

Then, somewhere along the way, your hands stop asking for permission.

You move faster. Think less.

And that’s when everything else starts to creep in.

The things you’ve been avoiding.
The conversations you haven’t had.
The decisions you keep pushing off because making them would make them real.

Some people order comfort.

Something warm. Familiar. Soft around the edges.

The kind of thing that feels like it might fix something… even if it doesn’t.

And for a few minutes, it does.

Other people don’t want comfort.

They want something that makes sense of the mess.

Something deeper. More complicated.

Because sometimes the simple answer isn’t the right one.

And sometimes… the right one doesn’t make sense at all.

Then there are the people who walk in like they already know.

No hesitation. No questions.

They’ve been here before.

They’ve made the hard calls.
They’ve lived with the outcomes.

And whether they got it right or not… they learned how to carry it.

And then there are the ones who are just trying to get through.

They don’t need soft.

They need something that works.

Something strong enough to hold them together until they can figure out what comes next.

For a moment… everything feels steady.

Like maybe you’ve got it under control.

Like maybe today is going to be the day where nothing goes wrong.

But it never lasts.

Because someone always changes something.

Adds more.
Takes something away.
Realizes too late they wanted something different.

And suddenly… you’re adjusting again.

That’s the thing about mornings.

They don’t ask you to be perfect.

They just ask you to show up.

To make the next thing in front of you… the best you can.

Even if you’re still figuring out everything else.

Because most of the time…

you don’t get clarity first.

You get responsibility.

And you learn the rest along the way.

This is where we are right now. This is the current rotation. This is what's showing now at The Sour Boule.
